# blenz 2.0 — Image Generation Prompts
**For Gemini Pro Image / Imagen / Midjourney / GPT-Image-1**

This document contains every image prompt for both Direction A and Direction B brand guideline pages, with **two unique alternatives per placeholder**, plus asset-attachment guidance for keeping the brand consistent across an image-gen session.

---

## How to use this document

**The workflow.** Open one chat per direction (one for A, one for B). At the start of that chat, attach all the brand assets you exported from Figma — the wordmark variants, the "smile after every sip" lockup, etc. Then paste the prompts in order, one section at a time. Most modern image models (Gemini 3 Pro Image, GPT-Image-1, Midjourney with cref/sref) will hold visual context across the chat if you keep referring back to "the wordmark I attached."

**The phrase that matters.** Every prompt below includes the line *"use the attached blenz wordmark"* or *"use the attached blenz secondary lockup"* — this is the trigger phrase that tells the model to pull from your uploaded assets instead of inventing typography. Don't remove it.

**One photoshoot, not 24 random shots.** Both directions need a consistent visual world. Same imaginary photographer, same location, same model casting, same time of day where possible. The prompts below are written so that if you generate them in order with the same model, they'll feel like one campaign.

---

## Universal style anchors

Paste this once at the start of your image-gen chat, before the first prompt. It primes the model.

> **Brand style guide for this session — blenz coffee, Direction [A/B]:**
> - Photography style: editorial lifestyle, half-magazine half-streetwear-lookbook. Think Aesop × Patta × Buttr brand campaigns.
> - Lighting: golden hour or hard north-window studio light. Never flat fluorescent. Never midday harsh.
> - Camera language: shot on a Hasselblad X1D II or Fujifilm GFX 100, 50mm or 80mm lens, shallow depth of field, slight film grain.
> - Colour grading: warm highlights, deep shadows, punchy yellows, slight desaturation in mid-tones.
> - Casting: Gen Z and young Millennial, multi-ethnic, Vancouver vibe, natural skin, no heavy makeup, candid expressions.
> - Wardrobe: contemporary basics — heavy cotton, denim, knit beanies, cream / off-white / burnt orange / deep navy palette.
> - Always upright wordmark, never italic. Always lowercase. The blenz brand never appears in uppercase.
> - When I say "use the attached wordmark," composite the exact wordmark I've uploaded — do not invent typography.
> - Output 4K, 4:5 portrait or 16:9 landscape unless otherwise specified.

After writing all 48 prompts and thinking through each direction's visual world: **lead with Direction A in your Sandeep presentation, but show B as the disciplined alternative.** Here's the honest reasoning:

# Image-Gen Workflow Tips

## On consistency across the document

Generate the **hero shot first** (IMG-01) — this sets the lighting, casting, location, and grade for the whole document. Once you have the hero, every subsequent prompt in that direction's chat can reference "the same lighting / casting / grade as the hero shot." This is how you get visual cohesion without micro-managing every prompt.

## On wordmark fidelity

No image model is currently perfect at typography. Even Gemini 3 Pro Image and GPT-Image-1 will sometimes mangle "blenz" into "blenza" or "bienz." Two safeguards:

1. **Always attach the wordmark as an asset.** Tell the model "use the attached wordmark, do not invent typography."
2. **Composite final wordmarks in Figma.** Generate the photo with a placeholder cup, then drop your real wordmark on top in Figma. This is the standard professional workflow — even big agencies do this. Your HTML2Design plugin makes this easy.

## On generating efficiently

Run prompts in **batches of 4** in the same chat. After the first batch, the model will have absorbed your brand language, style references, and typical compositions. Subsequent prompts will hit closer to target. Don't open a new chat per prompt — you'll lose all the visual context.

## On choosing between alternatives

For each placeholder I gave you a "selected" and "alternative" prompt. **Generate both.** Image-gen is a slot machine — you'll often find the alternative actually outperforms the selected. Pick the winner per slot, don't lock yourself into one prompt.
